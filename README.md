# Sajivni
Sanjivni: A Hybrid Telemedicine Platform for Rural Healthcare Sanjivni is a community-driven, hybrid (online-offline) telemedicine platform designed to bridge the healthcare gap in underserved rural communities. By creating a network of "Local Health Nodes" (LHNs) out of existing medical shops and clinics, we empower local communities to provide a first point of contact for healthcare and connect patients to a centralized hub of doctors and specialists.  The project directly addresses critical challenges such as long travel distances, limited access to specialists, poor internet connectivity, and financial strain on patients.  💡 The Core Concept Sanjivni operates on a simple but powerful principle: leverage existing local resources to provide hyper-local access to quality healthcare.  Local Health Nodes (LHNs): A network of registered local pharmacies, small clinics, and NGO kiosks. These are the community's healthcare access points.  Sanjivni Sahayaks: Trained staff at each LHN who assist patients, collect vital signs (BP, temperature, sugar level) with simple devices, and input data into the Sanjivni app.  Centralized Doctor Hub: A pool of government and private doctors who can conduct remote consultations via a dedicated web portal.
